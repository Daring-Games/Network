WAN

WAN's full form is Wide Area Network. This is the network that extends over a large geographical area ranging from more that 100km. This network has the primary purpose of computer networking. Since, people all over the world rely on data from various locations of the world. Internet can also be considered as a WAN.

This network is not tied up to a single location. This network is the most largest network to date. The customers can use this network no matter where they are but as long as they have WAN. Access to WAN is granted by different links such as VPN (Virtual Private Network). Without this network, organisations wouldn't exist as they wouldn't be able to grow.

ADVANTAGES

More Privacy

In WAN, we can share our personal data to our own websites without sharing it to the internet. If we encrypt our data before sending, by WAN. It adds an extra layer of protection. So, it is impossible to hack.

Updation

Wan network provides us with the ability to get updated files and data from the server. If the server is updated then all connected devices are also updated.

Sharing Data

Like PAN and LAN, in this network, we can share our data tp other users via internet.

High Bandwidth

Since WAN covers a large geographical area ranging from 100km to more than 1000km, it has a very high bandwidth as compared to other networks.

DISADVANTAGES

Expensive

Since WAN covers a large area at a geographical extent, it is very expensive at its initial state because it involves purchasing of different networking devices, i.e. routers, switches and extra security software.

Maintainence Issues

Once MAN network is set up, maintaining it is a full time job which requires high tech skills of network supervisors & technicians.

Troubleshooting Problems

A WAN network has many problems in fixing it. Most of the WAN wires go into the sea and if those are broken, it involves a lot of hard work to fix those lines under the sea.

An example of WAN is the Core Router.
This router resides within the backbone of the WAN. This router has the ability to forward IP packates at full speedon all interfaces. This also gives the WAN network its speed.
